【GL-Advcanced】Discussing work-life balance - 讨论工作和生活的平衡

发表于 2020-04-01 更新于 2020-04-14 分类于 EF ， GL ， Advanced

工作生活平衡对您来说以为着什么？想办法提高您的生活质量，并与全班分享您的想法。

Vocabularies of the course

  1. balance [ˈbæləns] n.均衡; 平衡; 均势; 平衡能力; 余额;
  2. balance [ˈbæləns] v.使(在某物上)保持平衡; 立稳; 相抵; 抵消; 同等重视(相对的两个事物或方面);
  3. regain [rɪˈɡeɪn] v.重新获得，恢复(能力或品质等); 回到(原位); 返回;
  4. regain balance 恢复平衡
  5. consequence(s) [ˈkɒnsɪkwənsɪz] n.结果; 后果; 重要性
  6. workaholic [ˌwɜːkəˈhɒlɪk] n.工作狂; 工作迷; 醉心于工作的人
  7. breakdown [ˈbreɪkdaʊn] n.(车辆或机器的) 故障，损坏; (关系的) 破裂; (讨论、系统的) 失败; 数字细目; 分类
  8. multitask [ˌmʌltiˈtɑːsk] v.多任务运行; 同时做数件事情;
  9. recharge your batteries 养精蓄锐; 你充电
  10. unwind [ˌʌnˈwaɪnd] v.解开，打开，松开(卷绕之物); 放松; 轻松;
  11. juggle [ˈdʒʌɡl] v.玩杂耍(连续向空中抛接多个物体); 尽力同时应付(两个或两个以上的重要工作或活动); 有效地组织，有效利用(信息、数字、开支等);
  12. the first sign 第一个信号
  13. the first sign of a breakdown 崩溃的第一个迹象
  14. terribly [ˈterəbli] adv.非常; 很; 非常地; 很厉害地;
  15. terribly important 非常重要
  16. comic strip character 连环画人物
  17. comic strip [ˈkɒmɪk strɪp] n.(常登载于报纸上的) 连环漫画
  18. strip [strɪp] v.脱光衣服; 脱掉大部分衣服; 扒光…的衣服; 进行脱衣表演; 表演脱衣舞; 除去，剥去(一层); (尤指) 剥光;
  19. strip [strɪp] n.(纸、金属、织物等) 条，带; (陆地、海域等) 狭长地带; 带状水域; 队服;
  20. proverb [ˈprɒvɜːb] n.谚语; 格言
  21. compromise(s) [ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪz] n.妥协; 折中; 互让; 和解; 妥协(或折中)方案; 达成妥协(或和解)
  22. compromise(s) [ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪz] v.(为达成协议而) 妥协，折中，让步; 违背(原则); 达不到(标准); (尤指因行为不很明智) 使陷入危险，使受到怀疑;
  23. the success of any single thing 任何一件事的成功
  24. frail [freɪl] adj.瘦弱的; 弱的; 易损的; 易碎的
  25. juggle the demands 应付各种要求
  26. prime minister [praɪm ˈmɪnɪstə(r)] n.总理; 首相
  27. Before long 不久以后”，相当于“soon,体现将来时态，是“不久以后、在短时间内、曾几何时”的意思，常与将来时、过去时态连用
  28. long before 很久以前 == long long ago
  29. life coach n.人生教练， 生涯顾问 (受雇帮他人实现人生和工作目标);
  30. imbalance [ɪmˈbæləns] n.失衡; 不平衡; 不公平;
  31. socialize [ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz] v.(和他人) 交往，交际; 使适应社会; 使社会主义化; 按社会主义原则行事;
  32. socialize with sb 与某人交往

Phrases of course

  1. It would benefit you in the long run to … 从长远来看它对你会有益的
  2. in the long run 从长远来看
  3. You should make an effort to … 你应该努力
  4. … will improve your quality of life.
  5. … could have serious consequences.可能造成严重后果
  6. I love to unwind and watch movies 我喜欢放松和看电影
  7. an increasing number of 越来越多; 越来越多的

Content of the course

  1. What does work-life balance mean to you?

    • important
    • sometimes pressure comes from work
    • keeping a balance helps us to have healthy life and healthy body
    • difficult to balance
    • difficult for women
    • pay attention to work and family

  2. Why is it important to have a balance between work and life?

    • better health
    • better relationships
    • better choices
    • fewer regrets
      What advice would you give someone to help them regain balance?
    • Write what you dont want
    • understand yourself
    • Remember that everything has two sides
    • Look at what you have lost as a result of imbalance
    • Remember what is most important
    • Go on a short holiday with your friends
    • Talk to someone who’s a good listener

  3. Did you advice look like any of these?
    What other pieces of advice can you give?

    • It would benefit you in the long run to …
    • You should make an effort to …
    • … will improve your quality of life.
    • … could have serious consequences.

  4. Fill in the graps wiht the appropriate word from the box. 

    - workaholic --- 5
- breakdown  --- 1
- multitask  --- 3
- recharge your batteries --- 6
- unwind --- 2
- juggle --- 4
    • 1.The first sign of a ___ is when you start thinking your work is terribly important.(Milo Bloom, American comic strip character)
    • 2.I love to ____ and watch movies.(Fred Savage, American actor)
    • 3.Try not to ____ if that compromises the success of any single thing.(Thai proverb)
    • 4.An increasing number of Canadians must ____ the demands of work with the need to care for children, of for family members who are ill or too frail to care for themselves.(Kim Campbell, Canadian prime minister)
    • 5.I’m a ____. Before long I’m traveling on my nervous energy alone.(Eva Gabor, Hungarian actress)
    • 6.Do things that make you laugh and ____.(Mary Pipher, American psychologist)

  5. You are seeking the advice of a life coach. Explain how you think your life is imbalanced.

    • Student A: Tell Student B (the life coach) how your work and life are imbalanced.
    • Student B: Give Student A advice on how they can improve their quality of life.
    • Example:
      • I don’t have any time to socialize with my friends.
      • It would benefit you in the long run if you made an effort to see your friends more.
