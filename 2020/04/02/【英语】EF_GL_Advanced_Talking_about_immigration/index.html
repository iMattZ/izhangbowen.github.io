Vocabularies of the course

  1. forever [fərˈevə(r)] adv.永远; 长久地; (与动词进行时连用) 老是，没完没了地;
  2. immigrant [ˈɪmɪɡrənt] n. (外来) 移民; 外侨;
  3. immigration [ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃn] n.移居(入境); 移民人数; 移民局检查站
  4. migration [maɪˈɡreɪʃn] n.迁移; 移居; 迁徙; (计算机系统的) 改变; (程序或硬件的) 迁移，转移;
  5. safety [ˈseɪfti] n.安全; 平安; 安全性; 无危险; 安全处所;
  6. employment n.工作; 职业; 受雇; 就业; 雇用
  7. skilled worker 熟练工
  8. asylum seeker [əˈsaɪləm siːkə(r)] n. 寻求避难者; 寻求庇护者
  9. refugee [ˌrefjuˈdʒiː] n.避难者; 逃亡者; 难民;
  10. primarily [praɪˈmerəli] adv.主要地; 根本地;
  11. asylum [əˈsaɪləm] n.(政治) 庇护，避难; 精神病院;
  12. stimulate [ˈstɪmjuleɪt] v.促进; 激发; 激励; 刺激; 使兴奋; 促进(身体某部分)的功能
  13. brain drain [ˈbreɪn dreɪn] n.(国家的) 人才流失;
  14. plug [plʌɡ] n.插头; (电源) 插座; 转换插头 v.堵塞; 封堵; 补足; 补充; 供给; 推广; 宣传;
  15. gap [ɡæp] n.开口; 豁口; 缺口; 裂口; 间断; 间隔; 间隙; 分歧; 隔阂; 差距
  16. plug the job gaps 填补空缺
  17. put on 穿; 戴; 涂; 抹; 上演; 举办; 展出; 增加（体重）; 打开（设备、装置等）; 播放（唱片、磁带或CD光盘）; 烹调; 加热; 对…下赌注; 押赌注于…; 提高…的成本（或价值）; （行为举止） 假装，矫饰;
  18. put demands on our resources 对我们的资源提出要求
  19. cheap labor 廉价劳力；工资低廉的工人
  20. labor [ˈleɪbə] n.劳动，努力；工作; 劳工，工人; 分娩，（分娩时的）阵痛
  21. debate [dɪˈbeɪt] v.(尤指正式) 讨论，辩论; 仔细考虑; 思考; 盘算
  22. argue against 为反对…而辩；反驳，否定
  23. migration - birds
  24. immigration - people

Phrases of course

  1. plug the job gaps 填补空缺

Content of the course

  1. Some people leave their countries forever. Why?

    • better living style
    • freedom
    • unify with family in other countries

  2. Describe the types of immigrants allowed into your country?

    • skilled worker
    • asylum seeker
    • refugee

  3. Which of these words did you use?

    • skilled worker
    • asylum seeker
    • refugee
    • employee
      Are they primarily looking for safety or employment?
    • safety
      • asylum seeker
      • refugee
    • employment
      • skilled worker
      • employee
        Identify other types of people involved in the immigration process.

  4. Loot at the statements below. Which argue for immigration? Which argue against?

    • Statement For Against
    • Skilled workers stimulate our economy.
    • When talented people leave, poor contries suffer ‘brain drain’
    • Immigrants help employers plug the job gaps.
    • Refugees may put demands on our resources
    • Immigrants provide cheap labor.

  5. Your city is debating whether to allow immigration this year.

    • Student A: You are arguing for immigration. Choose which types of immigrants you want to allow, and explain why.
    • Student B: You are arguing against immigration. Explain why.
      Example:
    • I am arguing for immigration. I want to allow all refugees and asylum seekers because we need to offer them protection from serious harm. I also…
    • I’m arguing against immigration. When refugees and asylum seekers enter a country, they put demands on resources. Furthermore,…

  6. Today we…

    • discussed why people immigrate.
    • used words related to immigrants and immigration issues.
    • argued for and against immigration.

  7. Before you go…

    • Describe your country’s immigration laws, Do you agree or disagree with them?
    • Talk about an immigrant you know. What benefits does the person provide to your country? Are there any problems?
