【GL-Advcanced】Making a budget - 做预算

发表于 2020-04-24 分类于 EF ， GL ， Advanced

Vocabularies of the course

  1. splash
  2. splurge
  3. idioms n.习语，惯用语，成语
  4. avoid + doing
  5. coupon
  6. squander v.浪费，挥霍（金钱，时间等）

Feedback and error

  1. I plan to avoid ** taking** my private car.
  2. I’m tring to go shopping less often. = I’m tring to shop less often.
  3. We need to put a cap on salaries.
  4. We nee to cut costs, such as teambuilding activities and business trips.
  5. perhaps we can outsource to save money.
  6. We must save for a rainy day because of the bad economy.

Content of the course

1. What can you do to save money?

  • take public transport
  • shop less
  • eat at home
  • get a bank account with a high interest rate
  • buy some stock
  • use coupons
  • stay at home
  • avoid parties

2. Talk about your personal budgeting goals for the year. What will you spend less on? What will you spend more on?

3. Did you use any of these phraes to talk about your budget? What other phrases can you think of?

  • to tighten your belt
  • to cost an arm and a leg
  • to splash out on something
  • to splurge on something = spend a lot of money on something
  • to cut back on
  • to put a cap on something = to set a limitation
  • to live within one’s means = spend money based on your salary.
  • to live below one’s means = spend less money than you have.
  • to keep your purse strings = control your spending
  • to squander = to waste
  • to cut down on little luxuries = stop buying unnecessary things
  • something set me back = it cost an arm and a leg
  • to save for a rainy day = save money in case of emergency
  • to be in the black = I don’t have any debt
  • to be in the red = I’m in debt

4. What do the folowing sentences mean?

  • The coat cost me an arm and a leg.
    • The coat cost me a lot of money.
  • I need to tighten my belt.
    • I need to control my spending.
  • I should cut down on those little luxuries.
    • I should stop buying unnecessary things.
  • It set me back quite a bit.
    • That cost me a lot of money.
  • I’m saving for a rainy day.
    • I want to keep some money in case of emergency.
  • I’m in the black.
    • I have no debt.
      Can you remember the phrases?

5. You and your colleague are setting the budget for your company.

  • Student A: Tell Student B what your company should cut back on.
  • Student B: Decide if you agree with Student A, and suggest what the company should spend more money on.
  • Example:
    • We should tighten our belt on advertising.
    • Of course, we don’t want to splash out on it, but advertising is still very important.
