【GL-Advcanced】Expressing sympathy - 表示慰问

发表于 2020-04-19 更新于 2020-04-23 分类于 EF ， GL ， Advanced

有些不好的事情发生在您的朋友身上。您知道如何安慰别人吗？练习一些常用短语。

Vocabularies of the course

  1. sympathy [ˈsɪmpəθi] n.同情; 赞同; 支持; 意气相投; 志同道合;
  2. We expressed our sympathy for her loss. 我们对她的损失表示同情.
  3. difficulties [ˈdɪfɪkəltiz] n.困难; 难题; 难事; 困境; 艰难; 费劲; 辛苦; 困难程度; 难度;
  4. the most common difficulties 最常见的困难
  5. go through 经历，经受（尤指苦难或艰难时期）; 翻阅; 翻找; 整理; 通读; 彻查; 走（流程或固定程序）; 例行做; （法律、协议等） 被通过; （正式决定） 被批准;
  6. go through a difficult time 经历一段困难时期
  7. mention [ˈmenʃn] v.提到; 写到; 说到 n.提及; 说起; 写上一笔
  8. condolence(s) [kənˈdəʊlənsɪz] n.吊唁; 慰唁
  9. disappointed [ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd] adj.失望的; 沮丧的; 失意的 v.使失望; 使破灭; 使落空;
  10. in a very serious situation 非常严重的局势
  11. in a less serious situation 不太严重的情况
  12. divorce [dɪˈvɔːs] n.离婚的男子,离婚; 分离; 脱离; v.与(某人)离婚; 判(某人)离婚; 使分离; 使脱离;
  13. Numerous marriages now end in divorce. 现在许多婚姻都以离婚收场。
  14. death in the family 家族中的死亡
  15. downsizing [ˈdaʊnsaɪzɪŋ] v.(公司、企业等) 裁员，精减
  16. learn [lɜːn] v.学; 学习; 学到; 学会; 听到; 得知; 获悉; 记住; 背熟; 熟记;
  17. just learned some terrible news 刚刚了解到一些严重的消息
  18. follow-up [ˈfɒləʊ ʌp] n.后续行动; 后续事物 adj.重复的; 补充的; 继续的; 接着的
  19. talk through their feelings 说出他们的感受
  20. talk through [tɔːk θruː] 详细讨论; 讨论清楚; 向…仔细讲解; 向…详细解释
  21. appropriate [əˈprəʊpriət] adj.合适的; 恰当的 v.盗用; 挪用; 占用; 侵吞; 拨(专款等);
  22. appropriately [ə’prəʊprɪətlɪ] adv.适当地；合适的；相称地;
  23. seriousness [ˈsɪəriəsnəs] n.严重; 认真; 严肃;
  24. the seriousness of 严重性
  25. bankruptcy [ˈbæŋkrʌptsi] n.破产
  26. redundancy [rɪˈdʌndənsi] n.(因劳动力过剩而造成的) 裁员，解雇; 多余; 累赘;
  27. unemployment [ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪmənt] n.失业; 失业人数; 无业; 没有工作;
  28. custody [ˈkʌstədi] n.监护; 保管; 监护权; 保管权; (尤指在候审时的) 拘留，拘押，羁押;
  29. I’m going to go to court to get custody of the children. 我将起诉，要求获得孩子们的监护权
  30. custody battle 争夺监护权
  31. battle [ˈbætl] n.战役; 战斗; 搏斗; 较量; 争论; 斗争; 奋斗; v.搏斗; 奋斗; 斗争;
  32. marital [ˈmærɪtl] adj.婚姻的; 夫妻关系的
  33. marriage [ˈmærɪdʒ] v.结婚; 婚姻; 婚姻生活; 已婚状态; 婚礼;
  34. In a good marriage, both husband and wife work hard to solve any problems that arise. 美满的婚姻中，夫妻双方会共同努力去解决出现的任何问题。
  35. insurance [ɪnˈʃʊərəns] n.保险; 保险业; 保险费; 保费;
  36. cure [kjʊə(r)] v.治愈，治好(病人或动物); 治好(疾病); 解决，了结(问题)
  37. An operation finally cured his shin injury. 手术最终治好了他胫部的伤。
  38. I can take you there. 我可以带你去那里。
  39. I can pick you up. 我可以接你
  40. I can give you a ride. 我可以载你一程。
  41. grieve [ɡriːv] v.(尤指因某人的去世而) 悲伤，悲痛，伤心; 使悲伤; 使悲痛; 使伤心;

#phrases of course

  1. I’m sorry for your loss.

  2. Please accept my condolences.

  3. I can really sympathize with you.

  4. I understand what you’re going through right now.

  5. I know how disappointed you must feel.

  6. You must really be disappointed.

  7. I’ll always be there for you. 我会一直支持你.

  8. If there’s anything I can do for you, let me know.

  9. What can I do for you?

  10. How can I help?

  11. It will get better with time. 随着时间的推移会好起来的。

Content of the course

1. What are some of the most common difficulties in life?

What might you say to someone who is going through a difficult time?

2. Did you mention any of these phrases to express sympathy?

  • I’m sorry for your loss.
  • Please accept my condolences.
  • I can really sympathize with you.
  • I understand what you’re going through right now.
  • I know how disappointed you must feel.
    Which phrases would you use in a very serious situation? Which might you use in a less serious situation?

3. Which of these life difficulties is the most serious? Which is the least serious?

  • divorce
  • death in the family
  • downsizing

Which of these phrases would you use to express sympathy in each situation above?

  • I understand what you’re going through.
  • Please accept my condolences.
  • You must really be disappointed.

4. You just learned some terrible news.

  • Student A: Tell Student B what happened.
  • Student B: Express sympathy for Student A’s experience.Ask follow-up questions to help your friend talk through their feelings.

5. Today we…

  • discussed some of life’s difficulties.
  • expressed sympathy appropriately, according to the seriousness of a situation.

6.Before you go…

  • How do people express sympathy in your country?
  • Do you send a card or flowers?
  • Do you spend more time with that person so they won’t feel lonely, or do you spend less time with them so they can grieve privately?
  • Do you tell others about it ,or keep the information to yourself?
