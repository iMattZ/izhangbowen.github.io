发表于 2020-04-14 分类于 EF ， GL ， Advanced

您负责装饰您的新办公室。说说哪种风格会最受青睐？

Vocabularies of the course

  2. critic [ˈkrɪtɪk] n.批评家; 评论家; 评论员; 批评者; 挑剔的人
  3. artwork [ˈɑːtwɜːk] n.(书刊等上的) 插图，图片; (尤指博物馆里的) 艺术作品;

  5. offensive [əˈfensɪv] adj.冒犯的; 得罪人的; 无礼的; 极其讨厌的; 令人不适的; 攻击性的; 进攻性的
  6. tasteful [ˈteɪstfl] adj.高雅的; 雅致的; 优美的
  7. groundbreaking [ˈɡraʊndbreɪkɪŋ] adj.开创性的; 创新的; 革新的;
  8. trendy [ˈtrendi] adj. 时髦的; 赶时髦的
  9. cliched 陈词滥调; 列排; 陈腐的;
  10. contemporary [kənˈtemprəri] adj.属同时期的; 同一时代的; 当代的; 现代的 n.同代人; 同辈人; 同龄人;
  11. vulgar [ˈvʌlɡə(r)] adj.庸俗的; 粗俗的; 粗野的; 不雅的; 粗鲁的; 下流的;

  32. portrait [ˈpɔːtreɪt] n.肖像; 半身画像; 半身照; 详细的描述; 描绘; adj.竖向的; 纵向打印格式的;

demo1

  1. I am looking for a portrait and I hope that it’s a famous mode. I really like the fashion.
  2. We have Jack Ma’s portrait. He is a role model for many young people/ youngsters.
  3. I want put a piece of art in my kids bedroom. It will create a pleasant atmosphere for your children
  4. I’m going to move in my new office next week. I’m looking for a photo or art piece that is classical and tasteful.
  5. I believe it will give you a very nice kind of mood. That is environmentally friendly.
  6. It’s classic and beautiful.

Content of the course

1. Is it art？ 这是艺术吗

2. Look at the works of art. What do you think of each of them?

How might an art critic describe these works? 一个艺术评论家怎么描述这些作品

3. Did you use any of these words to describe the artwork? What other words can you add to the list?

  • revolutionary
  • offensive
  • tasteful
  • unique
  • groundbreaking
  • trendy
  • cliched
  • contemporary
  • classic

4. Match the words to their definitions.

  • cliched = has been done or said many times before
  • contemporary = connected with what is happening now
  • offensive = unsetting;vulgar
  • revolutionary = something that hasn’t been done before 以前没做过的事
  • tasteful = attractive and chosen for style and quality
  • trendy = popular for a short period of time
  • unique = one of a kind

5. You are looking to buy a new work of art for your new office.

  • Student A: Tell Student B what kind of art you are looking for.
  • Student B: Describe a piece of art to Student A that you think they might like.
  • Example:
    • I need something tasteful for my office. I definitely want to avoid anything offensive.
    • A very interesting piece just came in. It’s classic yet unique.

6. Today we …

  • Used adjectives to describe art.
  • discussed our favorite works of art.
  • bought a work of art for our new office.

7. Before you go…

  • Why do you think artists create controversial art?
  • Have you heard of Damien Hirst’s shark in formaldehyde or Marcel Duchamp’s Fountain? What do you think of these types of artwork?
