【GL-Advcanced】Living in the city - 在城市居住

发表于 2020-03-31 更新于 2020-04-02 分类于 EF ， GL

越来越多的人装转移到城市找工作。谈谈居住在大城市’pros and cons’。您更向往居住在哪里？

Vocabularies of the course

  1. city 城市
  2. town 小城镇
  3. village 村庄
  4. country (side) 乡村，更田园些吧
  5. rank [ræŋk] v.把…分等级; 属于某等级; 排列; 使排成行;
  6. rank n. (尤指较高的) 地位，级别; 军衔; 军阶; 警衔; 普通士兵
  7. rank adj. 难闻的; 恶臭的; (强调质量、状况等) 极端的，糟糕的; 疯长的;
  8. affordable [əˈfɔːdəbl] adj.买得起的; 价格合理的; 负担得起的;
  9. recreation [ˌriːkriˈeɪʃn] n.娱乐; 消遣; 娱乐活动; 游戏
  10. scenery [ˈsiːnəri] n.风景; 景色; 风光; 舞台布景;
  11. delicious Cusisine
  12. cuisine [kwɪˈziːn] n.烹饪; 风味; (通常指昂贵的饭店中的) 饭菜，菜肴;

Phrases of course

  1. lots of job opportunities 很多工作机会
  2. affordable housing prices 经济适用房价格
  3. low cost of living 低生活成本
  4. excellent public transportation 优秀的公共交通
  5. fantastic weather 奇妙的天气
  6. low crime rate 低犯罪率
  7. other areas for recreation 其他娱乐区
  8. would rather do A than do B 宁愿……而不愿……
  9. would do A rather than do B 宁愿……而不愿……

Content of the course

  1. Would you like to live in a big city? Why or why not?

  2. Describe your city, town or village and the reasons you like living there.
    What is the biggest city you have ever been to?
    Name one thing you liked or disliked about it.

  3. Did you talk about any of these factors?

    • lots of job opportunities
    • affordable housing prices
    • low cost of living
    • excellent public transportation
    • fantastic weather
    • low crime rate

Rank these factors in order of importance to you.
Which are most important? Which are less important?
What other factors can you add to the ranking?
- close to parents
- beaches
- clean environment

  1. How do these factors describe the place where you live?
    Compare your answers with those of your classmates.
    • I agree strongly / I agree / I disagree / I disagree strongly.
    • The housing prices are affordable.
    • There’s excellent public transportation.
    • There are a lot of parks and other areas for recreation.
    • The people are friendly.
    • There’s a wide variety of shops and restaurants.

What are some negative factors about the place where you live?
For example, is the crime rate high? Is there a lot of pollution?

  1. Imagine you are talking to a friend about the city you’d most like to live in. Discuss the reasons you would like to live there.

    • Student A: Suggest a city to Student B and explain what factors make it attractive to you.

    • Student B: React to Student A’s suggestion and offer your own opinion about a city you’d like to live in.

      Example:

    • I’d like to live in New York. Job opportunities are most important to me, and there are plenty of jobs.

    • Yes, but the cost of living is really high, and so are housing prices. I’d rather live in Dublin. The people are so friendly.

  2. Today we …

    • talked about the benefits and drawbacks of city living.
    • discussed reasons for choosing a city to live in.
    • ranked factors of city living in order of importance to us.

  3. Before you go…

    • Have you ever heard this expression？ ‘It’s a nice place to visit, but I wouldn’t want to live there.’ Name a city you would like to visit but wouldn’t want to live in. Say why.
    • Make a list of the top three cities in the world. Name a characteristic that is important to you for each city.
